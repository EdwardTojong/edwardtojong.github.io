🧭 Who am I outside of work?

I’m the guy who refused to believe that a 9-to-5 grind is the only path. The one who went from customer service scripts to closing enterprise VoIP deals — doubling, then tripling my salary not by luck, but by learning how to sell value, not time.

I chase discomfort. Hiked the tallest mountain in South Korea. Lived out of a backpack across 11 countries in 24 months. Because every “no” on a sales call feels smaller after you’ve climbed a volcano at sunrise.

Why it matters? The motivation behind the man: I sell because I’ve been on the other side — underpaid, overworked, treated as replaceable. Sales gave me leverage. It gave me freedom. And now, I use it to help others break the same chains.

⚡ Continuous learning: juggled upskilling (AWS, HubSpot, SMstudy) while managing side-hustles (real estate, VA projects). Now I’m grounding myself: co-managing a retail microbusiness, finishing a house we bought 3 years ago, and prepping for the next chapter — fully remote, fully intentional.
